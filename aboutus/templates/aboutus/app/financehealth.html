Key Performance Indicators

Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR): A measure of a bank's capital. CAR is the ratio of a bank's capital to its risk-weighted assets. Higher CAR indicates better financial stability.

Return on Assets (ROA): An indicator of how profitable a bank is relative to its total assets. Higher ROA indicates efficient management of assets.

Non-Performing Assets (NPA): Loans or advances that are in default or arrears. Lower NPA indicates better asset quality and financial health.

Net Profit: The actual profit after working expenses not included in the calculation of gross profit have been paid. It indicates the overall profitability of the bank.
