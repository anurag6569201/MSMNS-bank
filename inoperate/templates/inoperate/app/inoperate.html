According to the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) guidelines, an account is classified as inoperative or dormant if there have been no transactions for over two years. Customers are advised to reactivate their inoperative accounts by visiting the bank with valid identification and address proof. The RBI mandates that banks should not levy charges for activation and should not impose any restrictions on the deposit of money in such accounts.
